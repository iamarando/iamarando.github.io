capsulae, 2018

glass, spelt


for the assignment that proposed a hypothetical
sculpture for the outer space that would be
propelled into the universe, i decided to work
with the theme of a potential human and
terrestrial message out there. my aim was to
condense the history of the human civilisations
into one object, while simultaneously referring
to the biosphere of the planet.

there the occurrence of the agricultural
revolution arose — as the milestone in known
history that marks the acceleration of human
development in the civilisational sense and the
first step into the profound changes that the
human species would cause in the environment.
therefore, each of the bubbles, made out of
glass, coming from the sand of the earth is
protecting a single grain of spelt — the

ancient grain, the ancestor of the contemporary
wheat. it comes to signify the first
domesticated plant — the manipulation of
steppes and meadows into fields that feed and
ultimately destroy. agriculture might represent
the universal sign in the language of
contemporary civilisation based in the initial
transition, which might even be framed as
liberation from the physical struggles of the
hunter-gatherer society through the means
of domestication.

it would like to ask what was the path between
the first grain that was planted with the
intention of sustaining and the peak
anthropocene of today. regardless of whether
the era of humans starts with the plant and end
with the coal, it cannot avoid the association
with the myth of the flood that might be coming.

this ark made of glass, demonstrating our
ability to alchemically transform the solidity
of the minerals of the lithosphere into a fluid

translucency, carries the sign of our rise that
might lead to the utter self-destruction.
the seed becomes the wanderer, weighted down by
the burden of being the greatest blessing and
possibly the gravest curse.
