impermanence inverted, 2018

plaster, bandages


with the edible inside of the pomegranate, what
remains is the peel — the negative imprint of
what once existed. dozens of fertile seeds
enveloped in irresistible bursting fructose.

the image of the pomegranate retains all its
mythical and symbolic associations — the fruit
of birth and fertility, yet the fruit that can
unrepairable bound the one it seduces to the
eternity of the underworld. with its
disappearance, the shadow its left, becoming a
carrier of its negative presence — the viola
that is left where it once existed. it is this
negative space that is used as a locus of new
growth — in this case, an artificially
stimulated recreation, the re-crystallisation
of the presence disappeared.

the newly occupied space, susceptible to the
organic remain of the peel, becomes the ground
for the natural processes of decomposition and
degradation of the remaining original matter
while embodying the essence of what is
inevitably disappearing. it becomes the
plasticity of what is being longed for.

the object itself becomes a hybrid between
what is inherited through the natural form used
and the human manipulation, and once liberated
from the hardened peel it can be distanced
from the essence of the fruit. it transgresses
from the particular specimen into the general
domain of the organic, becoming a deconstructed
ear of corn or the bodily in its familiarity.
it establishes itself as a micro biosphere,
providing the platform for the growth of
uncanny microorganisms, further visualising
the fragile relationship between the visible
and the individually imperceptible.
