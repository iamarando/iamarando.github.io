Lamija Čehajić (b.1997, Bosniak) is a young
aspiring artist who is interested in both
performance and object-oriented art, searching
for ways to merge them into a coherent whole.
as she was born in Prague but had to move to
Sarajevo as a child, her practice is informed
by a sense of displacement or lack, by a lack
of grounding and a confusion between
traditional Bosnia rooted values and her own
needs.
after graduating high school, she is currently
enrolled in Prague College at the department of
Fine Art, in a wide programme that encompasses
both traditional and new media, with an
emphasis on a strong conceptual and theoretical
backbone. she is trying to bend the digital to
communicate subtle concepts and gentle
aesthetics that she is inclined towards. she is
exploring the further fragmentation of the
narrative in the 21st century, loss of
continuity and linearity, using various media
that correlate to these themes.
recently, Lamija has participated in
alternative theatre projects in collaboration

with established artists and directors which
further perpetuated the performative aspect of
her work. she has been a part of a devised
theatre piece Stereopresence (created by
Cristina Maldonado with Sodja Zupanc Lotker,
at Alfred ve Dvoře theatre, Prague), which
lead her to participate in a collaborative
site-specific performance for the 4+4 Days in
Motion Festival 2017.
